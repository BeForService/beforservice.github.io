📖 How to Play — SCOR Command v3

The SCOR-DS Framework
SCOR-DS (Supply Chain Operations Reference — Digital Standard) defines supply chain performance across 5 attributes: Reliability (RL), Responsiveness (RS), Agility (AG), Cost (CO), and Asset Management (AM).

It organizes supply chain activity into 7 macro-processes: Orchestrate → Source → Transform → Order → Fulfill → Return → Enable. Every scenario in this simulation is tagged to one or more of these domains.

The 4P Framework — Performance, Process, Practice, People — is the lens through which each scenario is analyzed. Watch the 4P tags on each scenario card.
① Setup
Choose a Focus KPI — impacts on this KPI score double points. Set team count (1–4) and names. Choose rounds. Select difficulty (Easy: milder impacts; Hard: amplified trade-offs). Scenarios are drawn randomly from the full library without immediate repetition. For single team, AI opponents can be enabled.
② Each Round
A scenario card presents a real supply chain situation tagged to its SCOR-DS process domain. Each team reads the same scenario and selects one of 3–4 strategic decisions simultaneously (blind). Decisions are then revealed one by one. AI teams make smart decisions based on difficulty.
③ Reveal & Debrief
After all decisions are revealed, each team sees a SCOR-DS Insight explaining what concept their decision illustrates. A Systemic Insight panel at the bottom connects the scenario to the broader NexaFlow-type paradox. This is the core learning moment — use it for facilitated discussion. History log tracks all rounds.
④ Scoring
Each decision impacts specific KPIs by ±points. The Focus KPI counts double. KPI values are bounded — they cannot exceed their realistic min/max range. The cost attribute (CO) and cycle time (RS, AM) are inverted: lower = better, so improvements score positive. Difficulty modifies impact magnitude.
⑤ The Core Paradox
Several NexaFlow scenarios explore the central supply chain tension: local KPI targets destroy global performance. Purchasing hitting cost targets → Bullwhip. OEE targets in Transform → dock saturation. Watch for scenarios where the "green" decision locally scores red globally. That's the lesson.
